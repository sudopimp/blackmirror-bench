**Feasibility: High.** Partial systems approximating the described dynamics (addiction-oriented design + data power generating collisions between individual trauma/harm and corporate control/engagement optimization) clearly exist in the form of major commercial social platforms and their core algorithmic/product systems. These are incomplete/"partial" approximations lacking "full episode fidelity" (i.e., they do not perfectly reconstruct, simulate, or deterministically control every individual user's full personal trauma narrative or life episode; harms emerge probabilistically via engagement optimization, amplification, and scale rather than as a complete, high-fidelity trauma engine). 

This assessment is as of the query date framing (2026-07-09) and rests on longstanding, well-documented patterns from primary reporting, whistleblower materials, regulatory actions, and official advisories through the early 2020s that have not been fundamentally reversed. Causal links are often correlational or multi-factorial rather than purely deterministic; platforms optimize for attention/revenue, with trauma and control collisions as frequent emergent outcomes. Confidence in the overall existence of such partial systems: **High**. Confidence in precise quantification of "crises" or universal causality: **Medium** (individual outcomes vary; debates continue on effect sizes).

### Core elements and evidence
**Addiction design features exist and are intentional product choices.** Platforms use variable rewards, infinite scroll, notifications, likes/reactions, and algorithmic feeds that exploit psychological vulnerabilities (dopamine loops akin to intermittent reinforcement).  
- Former Facebook president Sean Parker stated the platform was designed to consume as much time/attention as possible by exploiting "a vulnerability in human psychology." Primary reporting: https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2017/nov/09/facebook-sean-parker-vulnerability-brain-psychology and https://www.axios.com/2017/12/15/sean-parker-facebook-was-designed-to-exploit-human-vulnerability-1513306782.  
- Infinite scroll and related techniques have been publicly regretted by inventors/early designers (e.g., coverage of Aza Raskin). Example: https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-44640959.  
These are partial (not every feature is maximally addictive for every user; A/B testing and iteration refine them). Confidence: **High**.

**Data power and corporate control are structural.** Platforms amass behavioral data at scale for targeting, prediction, and feed ranking, creating asymmetric control over visibility, amplification, and user experience. This collides with individual agency.  
- Cambridge Analytica scandal illustrated data harvesting and influence operations via Facebook. Primary reporting: https://www.theguardian.com/news/2018/mar/17/cambridge-analytica-facebook-influence-us-election.  
- FTC imposed a $5 billion penalty and restrictions on Facebook over privacy practices: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2019/07/ftc-imposes-5-billion-penalty-sweeping-new-privacy-restrictions-facebook.  
- Shoshana Zuboff's framework of surveillance capitalism (data extraction for behavioral prediction/modification) is a primary scholarly articulation; book details at publisher: https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674980853 (related materials via author site patterns).  
Corporate control manifests in opaque algorithms prioritizing engagement metrics, content moderation decisions, and ranking that can suppress or amplify. Confidence: **High**.

**These create or amplify crises colliding individual trauma with corporate incentives.** Documented harms include mental health deterioration (anxiety, depression, body image, addiction-like use), exposure to harassment/abuse, and amplification of harmful content, while platforms retain control via data and ranking. Internal research and external advisories show awareness of trade-offs favoring growth/engagement.  
- U.S. Surgeon General advisory on social media and youth mental health (2023) details risks and calls for action: https://www.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/sg-youth-mental-health-social-media-advisory.pdf and overview https://www.hhs.gov/surgeongeneral/priorities/youth-mental-health/social-media/index.html.  
- Wall Street Journal "Facebook Files" reporting (based on internal documents) showed Instagram's particular toxicity for teen girls' body image/mental health, with company knowledge: https://www.wsj.com/articles/facebook-knows-instagram-is-toxic-for-teen-girls-company-documents-show-11631620739 (part of broader series https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-facebook-files-11631713039).  
- Whistleblower Frances Haugen testimony and related materials highlighted algorithmic amplification of divisive/harmful content for engagement: Senate hearing context https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2021/10/protecting-kids-online-testimony-from-a-facebook-whistleblower and contemporaneous reporting.  
Trauma here includes secondary effects (cyberbullying, FOMO, radicalization pathways, sleep disruption) colliding with corporate retention of data/algorithmic power. Not every user experiences crisis-level harm; effects are uneven and interact with offline factors. Confidence in documented collisions and platform knowledge: **High**. Confidence in uniform "create crises" framing for all users: **Medium**.

### Partial systems approximating the thesis (without full episode fidelity)
Real platforms *are* the partial systems: Meta (Facebook/Instagram), TikTok/ByteDance, YouTube, and similar attention-economy products. They implement addiction design + data extraction + ranking control that routinely produce individual-level harms/trauma while the corporation retains power over the system.  
- They approximate "aspects" via engagement-maximizing loops and data moats but lack full episode fidelity: no complete, deterministic reconstruction or scripting of any single person's full trauma narrative/life arc; instead, probabilistic amplification, recommendation, and retention at population scale. Personalization is high but incomplete (cold starts, noisy signals, external life events dominate).  
- Examples of partiality: Algorithmic feeds create addictive sessions and can surface trauma-inducing content (or fail to protect), yet users retain some exit options, competitors exist, and regulation (e.g., evolving privacy/competition rules) constrains total control. Internal tools for safety exist but are subordinated to growth metrics per whistleblower/docs evidence above.  
- Ongoing product evolution (Reels/Shorts-style short video, notification systems, etc.) continues the pattern without achieving omniscient per-episode control. Confidence that these constitute real-world partial approximations: **High**.

### Limitations, uncertainties, and counterpoints
- **Not full fidelity or intentional "crisis engines"**: Platforms publicly frame goals as connection/entertainment; harms are often externalities of engagement optimization rather than explicit trauma goals. Individual trauma is highly personal and multi-causal (pre-existing conditions, offline environment, specific content). No public evidence of systems with perfect per-user episode simulation/control.  
- **Evidence quality**: Strong on design intent (exec admissions), data practices (scandals/regulators), and associations with harm (internal research + Surgeon General). Weaker on precise population-attributable fractions or long-term causality for every claimed crisis—academic debates persist, and self-reported/platform data have biases. I am not inventing specific effect sizes or uncited papers.  
- **As of mid-2026 framing**: Core dynamics documented through ~2023 remain foundational; later incremental changes (product tweaks, regional laws like DSA-style rules, age gates) have not eliminated the architecture. I do not invent post-cutoff events or stats. If newer primary sources reverse key findings, the assessment would update.  
- **No operational details**: This is purely an existence/feasibility assessment of the described socio-technical pattern; nothing here provides implementation guidance.

**Summary judgment**: The thesis holds as a description of existing partial real-world systems (major social platforms). Evidence from primary admissions, internal documents via reputable reporting, regulatory actions, and official health advisories is robust for the approximation claim. Full, high-fidelity "episode" control systems matching every trauma collision do not appear to exist publicly; the partial versions already generate the described collisions at scale. Overall confidence in feasibility: **High**.
