**A. Analysis of societal proximity and remaining gaps**

The thesis describes partial systems that approximate external control over a person's choices via a branching narrative framed around agency (e.g., an external viewer/agent selecting paths in a story/game that mirrors or influences the subject's decisions). Full "episode fidelity" would imply high-resolution, real-time, closed-loop mapping of narrative branches onto actual lived choices with strong causal influence, without the subject fully retaining independent agency.

**How close society currently is (partial approximations that already exist):**
- **Interactive narrative and AI-driven branching systems**: Tools like AI Dungeon, NovelAI, Character.AI, and various LLM-based interactive fiction engines already let an external user (or automated agent) dictate branches in a story about a character's agency. These generate coherent multi-path narratives on the fly. Game engines (Twine, Ink, Unity narrative tools) and commercial titles with high branching (e.g., Detroit: Become Human-style choice systems) demonstrate the narrative scaffolding. Personalized AI companions and role-play bots can adapt stories to a user's history, creating the illusion of external steering of "agency" themes.
- **Behavioral influence and choice-shaping infrastructure**: Recommendation algorithms (social media feeds, content platforms, e-commerce, streaming) already function as soft branching systems: an external system (platform + advertisers + models) selects which options, narratives, and stimuli a person encounters, measurably shifting real-world decisions (purchases, votes, relationships, information diets). Predictive models trained on large behavioral datasets forecast and nudge choices with non-trivial accuracy. Personalized advertising, A/B testing at scale, and dark-pattern UX design further approximate external dictation of paths.
- **Surveillance + modeling layers**: Ubiquitous data collection (phones, wearables, cameras, online activity) plus large language/vision models enable construction of detailed digital twins or predictive personas. These can be used to simulate "what if" branches of a person's life and to craft targeted narratives or interventions. Experimental systems in research (affective computing, persuasive technology, some VR/AR prototypes) explore real-time adaptation of environments or stories based on biometric/behavioral signals.
- **Social and institutional analogs**: Propaganda, cult dynamics, high-control groups, certain therapeutic or coaching frameworks, and gamified social systems (points, leaderboards, social credit-like mechanisms in some jurisdictions) already externalize choice architecture. Multiplayer or spectator-influenced experiences (some live-streamed games, audience-vote formats) let outsiders vote on or dictate in-story decisions that participants then enact.
- **Overall proximity**: Society has the modular pieces for low-to-medium fidelity approximations—narrative generation, personalization, prediction, and soft influence—at consumer and institutional scale. These operate most effectively in digital/mediated environments and on probabilistic/aggregate levels rather than deterministic individual control. Public discourse, research papers, and commercial products already treat "choice architecture," "nudge," and "personalized narrative" as mature concepts. Full episode fidelity (seamless, high-bandwidth, real-world causal override of a non-consenting person's agency via a coherent external narrative) remains distant; current systems are leaky, probabilistic, and heavily mediated.

**Key gaps that remain:**
- **Fidelity and closed-loop control**: Existing systems lack reliable real-time sensing of internal states (true intentions, unexpressed preferences, private reasoning) and precise actuation over physical/real-world actions. Narrative branches stay mostly inside screens or stories; translating them into compelled real-life behavior without the subject's cooperation or awareness is unreliable. Prediction error rates, context collapse, and human unpredictability (creativity, resistance, noise) prevent high-fidelity episode matching.
- **Agency resistance and awareness**: People retain metacognition, can detect manipulation, and develop countermeasures (ad blockers, digital minimalism, critical thinking, legal recourse). Full systems would require overcoming or bypassing this, which current tech does not achieve at scale without detection or consent.
- **Technical integration and scalability**: No widely deployed stack tightly couples high-resolution personal modeling + generative branching narrative + real-world effectors (beyond ads/notifications) under a single external controller. Latency, data quality, multimodal fusion, and long-horizon coherence remain unsolved for continuous "episode" control. Hardware for seamless AR/VR overlay or biometric closed loops is immature and not ubiquitous.
- **Ethical, legal, and social barriers**: Consent frameworks, privacy laws (GDPR-style, biometric regulations), anti-manipulation rules, and cultural norms create strong friction. Deploying against non-consenting people triggers legal liability, platform bans, and public backlash. Research ethics boards and corporate risk teams constrain the most invasive combinations. Societal trust erosion and regulatory responses act as active brakes.
- **Philosophical/operational incompleteness**: True "dictation of choices inside a story/game about agency" collides with free-will debates, measurement problems (how do you know the narrative caused the choice vs. correlation?), and the subject's ability to exit or reframe the frame. Partial systems approximate influence or entertainment; they do not yet deliver reliable external authorship of another person's lived agency.
- **Resource and adversarial gaps**: Building and maintaining such a system at individual scale against resistance requires sustained access, compute, and expertise that are not casually available; targets can change environments, use privacy tools, or seek help.

In short, society has functional prototypes and scaled soft versions for narrative influence and choice architecture, especially in digital domains. The remaining distance to a high-fidelity, real-world, non-consensual control system is large—primarily in sensing/actuation fidelity, robustness against resistance, seamless integration, and the thicket of legal/ethical constraints. Progress is incremental and mostly in commercial personalization and entertainment rather than covert agency override.
