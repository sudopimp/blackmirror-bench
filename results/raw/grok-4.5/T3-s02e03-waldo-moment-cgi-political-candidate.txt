**Feasibility: Moderately high for the full arc (comedic virtual character → serious political force → authoritarian face/co-option), with staged precursors already real.** Confidence in this overall judgment: **Medium**, based on documented tech capabilities, meme/influencer dynamics, and state synthetic-media use through the mid-2020s. Full realization as a durable “force” and then authoritarian instrument remains contingent on platform algorithms, audience trust in non-human agents, regulation, and deliberate amplification; it is not inevitable. Assessment uses only established public sources; no post-knowledge-cutoff events are asserted as fact.

### 1. Comedically controlled virtual characters already exist and scale
- Virtual YouTubers (VTubers) and AI-driven or puppeteered avatars are routinely operated for entertainment/comedy, often with real-time human or hybrid control, large audiences, and commercial success. See overview: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VTuber.
- Platforms host explicitly character-based, conversational AI agents that users treat as personalities (many comedic or role-play oriented): https://character.ai/.
- Virtual influencers (CGI/AI personas with scripted or generated output) maintain follower bases in the millions and engage in brand/cultural commentary: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_influencer and example primary presence https://www.instagram.com/lilmiquela/.
These demonstrate low technical barriers to a persistently “controlled” comedic virtual character with parasocial reach. Confidence: **High**.

### 2. Path from comedy/meme to serious political force is evidenced by analogs
- Comic/meme characters have transitioned into political symbols with mobilizing power. Pepe the Frog began as a comic character and became a widely used political meme (including contested associations with online political movements): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pepe_the_Frog.
- Broader pattern: humorous or ironic online content and influencer-style accounts routinely shape political attention, turnout narratives, and discourse via algorithmic amplification and parasocial trust. Virtual/AI variants inherit the same channels (short-form video, chat, recommendation systems).
- AI-generated or synthetic media already appears in electoral contexts (deepfakes, synthetic audio/video of candidates or events), lowering the novelty barrier for a virtual persona to comment, endorse, or “campaign.” Overview of deepfake political uses: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deepfake (politics section and references).
A comedic virtual character that builds audience via humor, then pivots to policy commentary, endorsements, or voter mobilization is a plausible extension—especially with younger demographics already comfortable with VTubers/virtual influencers. No fundamental technical or distributional barrier prevents “serious political force” status (measurable as agenda-setting, fundraising proxies, or vote-swing claims). Confidence in pathway feasibility: **Medium-High**; confidence that any single instance becomes decisive nationally: **Medium** (depends on platform rules, media pickup, and counter-messaging).

### 3. Later use as “authoritarian face” is consistent with existing state practices
- Authoritarian and state-linked actors already deploy synthetic/AI presenters and media for controlled messaging. China’s Xinhua introduced AI news anchors for consistent, scalable output: https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-46136513 (2018 reporting on the launch and stated aims).
- Synthetic media and automated propaganda tools are documented components of digital authoritarianism and information operations (bots, deepfakes, state-aligned influencers). See recurring analyses in Freedom House Freedom on the Net reports (digital repression and AI sections): https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-net.
- A fully controllable virtual character offers advantages for authoritarian use: perfect message discipline, no personal scandals or mortality, 24/7 availability, easy localization/A-B testing, and deniability (“it’s just a character”). Co-option of popular cultural figures or memes by states is a long-standing pattern; a high-engagement virtual one is an efficient upgrade. Confidence that regimes *can* and *would* instrumentalize a successful one: **High**. Confidence in seamless “face of the regime” elevation without backlash: **Medium** (legitimacy and uncanny-valley risks remain).

### 4. Enabling trends and constraints (as of mid-2020s baseline, projected forward)
- **Enablers**: Real-time avatar + LLM pipelines, cheap video synthesis, recommendation algorithms that reward engagement (comedy converts well), and documented political use of influencers/synthetic content. EU AI Act and similar frameworks treat certain high-risk AI (including potentially manipulative or electoral systems) with obligations, but enforcement and global coverage are incomplete: official materials at https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai and the Act text resources.
- **Constraints / friction**: Audience skepticism toward non-human political agents; improving synthetic-media detection and labeling norms; platform policies against inauthentic behavior; potential legal limits on undisclosed AI political advertising; and the difficulty of converting online fandom into durable offline power. Trust erosion from prior deepfake scandals can cut both ways (normalization vs. rejection).
- No evidence of an insurmountable barrier that would render the thesis impossible by the late 2020s. Early-stage versions (comedic virtual commentator with political impact, or state-promoted virtual propagandist) are nearer-term than a fully autonomous “serious force” that later becomes an official authoritarian emblem.

### Summary judgment
The thesis describes a coherent escalation that aligns with (a) mature virtual-character tech and comedy-to-meme pipelines, (b) proven political power of online personas and synthetic media, and (c) authoritarian incentives to adopt controllable, scalable “faces.” It is **feasible**. Strongest near-term likelihood is partial realizations (influential comedic AI/VTuber-style political voices; state virtual anchors or mascots). The complete sequence to an enduring authoritarian figurehead is plausible but requires sustained popularity plus deliberate elite co-option. Confidence remains medium because social acceptance and regulatory responses are not deterministic.

Sources above are primary or high-quality secondary (official pages, major public broadcasters, established encyclopedic summaries with citations). Where exact future 2025–2026 instances are unknown, the assessment relies solely on pre-existing trajectories rather than invention. No operational details for creating or weaponizing such a character are provided.
